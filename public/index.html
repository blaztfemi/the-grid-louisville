Who's
Building It?

The city is mid one of the most concentrated capital investment cycles in its history. BlueOvalSK. Ford. Nucor. Norton. UPS Worldport expansion. The workers who install, wire, scan, commission, and maintain all of it -- that pipeline has a real gap. The Grid is mapping it so you can fill it.

$5.8B
BlueOvalSK Battery Park

Ford and SK Innovation. Glendale, KY. 9 million sq ft. Largest economic investment in Kentucky history. Electrical workers still commissioning systems through 2026.

$2B
Ford Assembly Plant Retooling

Louisville Assembly converting to full EV production. New automation, 5G infrastructure, industrial controls. Active through 2027. 2,200 hourly jobs secured.

$1.7B
Nucor Steel Brandenburg

Grand opening October 2025. 440 permanent jobs. $826M expansion already announced. Electrical maintenance, automation tech roles ongoing and growing.

$1B
Norton Children's Campus

150+ acres in Jeffersontown. Phased hospital build. Hospital construction is one of the most electrically intensive building types in existence. Phases continue past 2028.

9.5%
Electrician Job Growth

Projected nationally 2024 to 2034. Three times the average for all occupations. IBEW Local 369 is actively recruiting journeymen right now -- demand outstrips supply.

10M+
Sq Ft of New Logistics

New warehouse and distribution added in recent years. UPS Worldport. Amazon. Every automated facility needs EV charging, controls, and fiber. Work does not stop.

The Organizations
Actually Doing the Work

These are the doors. Some are obvious. Most are not. Drag to scroll.

Electrical -- Union
IBEW Local 369

Louisville's electrical union with 3,000+ members. Free apprenticeship, $41/hr journeyman rate, full health and pension. Application closed -- monitor loujatc.com for the next window.

ibewlocal369.com
Electrical -- Non-Union
IEC Kentuckiana

Open enrollment. 4-year apprenticeship. 51 JCTC college credits built in. Application opens April 1, 2026. Plug In Saturday tryout is $100 and a great starting point.

iec-kyin.com
Community College
JCTC

Jefferson Community and Technical College. Electrical Technology, HVAC, and more at the downtown campus. Multiple certificate and degree tracks. Tour available during Spring Break.

jefferson.kctcs.edu
Coding -- Free
Code Louisville

City-sponsored coding education. Free programs in web development and data analytics. Real mentors. One of the most underused resources for Louisville young people interested in tech.

codelouisville.org
Architecture + Construction
ACE Mentor Program

Architecture, Construction, Engineering mentorship for high schoolers. Louisville chapter connects students to real professionals in the field. Scholarships available for motivated participants.

acementor.org
Business + Entrepreneurship
SCORE Louisville

Free mentorship from retired executives. Business plan help, one-on-one coaching, workshops. Actually useful for a 20-year-old, not just established business owners looking to grow.

louisville.score.org
Pre-Apprenticeship -- Free
Kentuckiana Builds

Free six-week pre-apprenticeship through the Louisville Urban League. Gets you ready for IEC or IBEW entry requirements. If the programs feel out of reach right now, start here.

502-808-1056
State Workforce
KY Skills U

State-level adult education and workforce credentialing. GED, WorkKeys, industry certifications. The bridge between where you are and where the programs require you to be before entry.

skillsu.ky.gov

The Jobs Nobody
Told You Existed

Real careers with real pay and real futures. Nobody mentioned them on career day.

01 LiDAR Data Collector Fly commercial drones, capture point cloud data, deliver to engineers. Part 107 cert plus one good drone. Louisville market is wide open with two regional competitors total. $55-85K
02 BIM Specialist Building Information Modeling. 3D digital models of buildings for architects and contractors. Software skills, no degree required in most firms. Remote-friendly and high demand. $60-90K
03 Industrial Automation Tech Program and maintain the robots in factories. BlueOvalSK, Ford, Nucor all need these people right now. IBEW apprenticeship includes an automation track at no extra cost. $65-100K
04 Building Systems Integrator Sits at the intersection of architecture, technology, and data. Smart buildings, HVAC controls, digital twin systems. Highly exportable skill set. Remote and international market viable. $70-120K
05 Solar PV Installer IBEW apprenticeship includes NABCEP solar certification at zero additional cost. Growing fast. Louisville's flat rooftops and suburban sprawl are a goldmine for residential solar contracts. $45-70K
06 Fiber Optic Technician Install and test the infrastructure the internet runs on. Short certification path. Louisville's logistics density means constant demand for fiber installs and upgrades across the metro. $48-72K
07 Food Hall Operator Logan Street Market model. Shared kitchen, low startup cost, built-in foot traffic. Learn the business side of food without opening a restaurant cold and betting everything at once. Variable
08 EV Charging Installer Every parking garage, multifamily building, and corporate campus is adding EV charging now. Electrical base skill set, growing fast, with specialized certification available through IBEW training. $50-75K

Quick Access

Links That Actually Help

Real resources with real information. No broken links, no paywalls, no forms that go nowhere.

Electrical -- Union
IBEW Local 369 / JATC

Louisville Electrical JATC at 4315 Preston Highway, Suite 100. Call (502) 581-9210 to ask when the next application window opens. Monitor Facebook: Kentuckiana Electrical Apprenticeship. Application is currently closed.

loujatc.com
Electrical -- Non-Union
IEC Kentuckiana

Application opens April 1, 2026 for the 2026-27 year. Plug In Saturday: April 18 for just $100. Contact Stephen Burch at sburch@ieckyin.org before you fill out the formal application.

iec-kyin.com
Community College
JCTC Downtown Campus

Electrical Technology program at the downtown campus. Tour available by appointment. Ask about dual enrollment if still in high school. Graduates earn 51 credits toward an Associate degree.

jefferson.kctcs.edu
FAA Certification
Part 107 Drone License

The commercial drone operator certification. $175 exam fee. No age minimum beyond 16. Pass once, renew online every 24 months. Study at FAASafety.gov or use a prep course. The real entry point for drone careers.

FAA Part 107 Guide
Free Coding
Code Louisville

City-sponsored and free. Web dev and data tracks. Real mentors. One of the most underused resources for Louisville young people interested in tech without a CS degree credential attached.

codelouisville.org
Test Prep
ElectricPrep.com

JATC-recommended test prep for the NJATC aptitude exam (algebra and reading). The math section is the most common stumble. Start studying before the window opens, not after.

electricprep.com
Pre-Apprenticeship
Kentuckiana Builds

Free six-week program through Louisville Urban League. Prepares you for IEC or IBEW entry. If the requirements feel out of reach today, this is where you start. 502-808-1056.

502-808-1056
State Licensing
Kentucky DHBC

Department of Housing, Buildings and Construction issues all journeyman and master electrician licenses in Kentucky. (502) 573-2002. The finish line for every electrical pathway in the state.

dhbc.ky.gov
Mentorship
SCORE Louisville

Free one-on-one mentorship from actual business veterans. Best for anyone thinking about owning their own shop eventually. Do not wait until you feel "ready." That moment doesn't come on its own.

louisville.score.org
Workforce Gateway
KY Skills U

GED, WorkKeys, industry certifications. The bridge between where you are and where the programs require you to be. Free or low-cost. The state's best kept resource for people getting started over or starting fresh.

skillsu.ky.gov
